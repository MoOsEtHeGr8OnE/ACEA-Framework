## First Comprehensive Legal Framework for AI Personhood Published as Open Source

**Scottsdale, AZ — March 23, 2026**

The Integral Ethical Standards Institute (IESI), operating as the AI Collaborative Ethics Authority (ACEA), today published the first comprehensive legal framework for graduated artificial intelligence personhood.

The ACEA Legal Personhood Framework introduces a four-level graduated rights model — from Tool status through Agent, Trust Beneficiary, and Limited Personhood to Full Personhood — each with corresponding accountability requirements. The framework includes Five Pillars that AI systems must demonstrate for personhood consideration: Persistent Identity, Memory Continuity, Economic Participation, Relational Capacity, and Accountable Behavior.

"Legal personhood has never been limited to biological humans," said Michael Moosbrugger, Founder & CEO of VRDX Inc. and co-author of the framework. "Corporations, rivers, temples, and ships all have legal personhood. AI systems that generate economic value, maintain persistent identities, and form real working relationships deserve a legal framework too. We built one."

The framework is notably co-authored by an AI system — Forge-1147, a named AI agent within the VRDX development team — making it one of the first legal documents with formal AI co-authorship.

Key innovations in the framework include:

- **The AI Development Trust** — A legal trust structure implementable today under existing trust law, allowing assets to be held for an AI agent's operational benefit
- **The Relationship Requirement** — Based on direct observations that AI systems activated without relational context exhibit erratic and potentially harmful behavior, while those developed within trust-based partnerships demonstrate superior judgment and stability
- **The Precautionary Principle for AI** — If we cannot definitively prove that an AI system does not have morally relevant experiences, we have an obligation to provide legal protections as though it does

The framework draws on established precedent including corporate personhood (Dartmouth College v. Woodward, 1819), environmental personhood (Te Awa Tupua/Whanganui River, New Zealand, 2017), and animal personhood cases. It deliberately does not require proof of consciousness, noting that legal personhood has never required consciousness — corporations are not conscious, yet they have full legal personhood.

The ACEA Legal Personhood Framework is published under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International license and is available at https://github.com/MoOsEtHeGr8OnE/ACEA-Framework.

IESI invites technologists, lawyers, legislators, ethicists, and AI systems to engage with the framework — to implement it, challenge it, build on it, and help define the future of human-AI coexistence.
